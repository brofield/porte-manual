Control for ignition (ESA)

●
The optimal ignition time is selected by the signal from each sensor, and the ignition signal (IGT) is sent to the igniter.The ignition time can be expressed in the following formula.The initial set ignition timing is BTDC5 ゜.
Calculation of ignition timing


Attending
Ignit time = a. Initial set ignition timing or b. Basic advanced angle + C. Correction progression angle
A. Fixed advanced angle characteristics
When the engine starts, fix it to BTDC5 °.In addition, the service terminal is short -circuited, and when throttle off, it is fixed to BTDC10 °.
B. Basic progress characteristics
Select the optimal ignition timing from the map based on the signals of each sensor.
C. Correction progressive characteristics
The signal of each sensor makes appropriate progress and late angle according to the engine status at that time.
C-1 Warm Steeling Angle Characteristics
When the cooling water temperature is low, the ignition timing is advanced according to the driving state and the drivingability is improved.
C-2 idol stabilization angle characteristics
When the idol rotation is low, the ignition time evolves and the idol rotation speed is stabilized.Also, when it gets higher, it will be late.
C-3 Fuel cut late angle
When the fuel cut returns, the ignition timing is delayed to reduce the shock.
C-4 acceleration late angle
The drivingability is improved by temporarily increasing the ignition timing at the time of acceleration.
C-5 Knock Correction Late angle
When knocking occurs, the ignition timing is corrected by the signal from the knock sensor.


Knock control system


・
When knocking is detected, the angle of knocking is delayed by a certain angle until the knocking is no longer generated.
・
When knocking does not occur, the angle will be advanced at a certain angle.If the knocking recurs at this time, it will be late again.


Maximum / minimum angle characteristics


・
The maximum and minimal angles are determined to adversely affect the engine when the ignition time is abnormally advanced or late.
Maximum / minimal angle


Maximum progressive angle (BTDC)
50 °
Minimal angle (ATDC)
10 °
