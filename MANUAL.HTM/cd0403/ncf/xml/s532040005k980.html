Rotary tripride coupling

●
The rotary libred coupling is similar to the bisukus coupling in shape, but the mechanism is completely different.The Biscas Cup Ring is a mechanism that uses a torque transmission to transmit a faster response generated when silicone oil is refused, while the Rotary Revised Coupling is a mechanism that uses the pressure generated by silicone oil.
●
When the rotor of the rotary libred coupling rotates (differential) to the housing or piston, the silicone oil moves (flow) while being pushed on the front of the rotor.At that time, silicone oil is generated by silicone oil flow resistance between silicone oil and housing and piston.The generated pressure acts on the piston to push the wet multi -plate clutch and transmit torque.

●
The rotary librade coupling has an asymmetric rotor (blade) shape in order to generate pressure on one side of the rotor, and the size of the generated pressure and the size of the transmission torque depending on the rotation direction, so it is before ABS.The torque interference between the rear wheels is reduced.Therefore, a stable braking performance can be obtained while functioning as 4WD even when ABS is operating.

Rotary triprade coupling structure


・
The rotary triprade coupling consists of a housing, separator plate, friction plate, piston rotor (blade), etc.
・
The torque transmission clutch part is a wet multi -plate clutch.This wet multi -plate clutch has the same structure as the clutch structure for automatic transmission transmission, and the material is basically the same, and the automatic transmission fluid is filled.
・
The wet multi -plate clutch is assembled with no clearance, and the piston is hardly moved.For this reason, the hydraulic pressure generated by rotor (blade) rotation is instantly transmitted to the clutch portion.
・
The pressure occurring part is filled with high -viscosity silicone oil, and the rotated rotor is changed in the shape of the straight turn and the reverse direction.
・
The housing and piston are made of aluminum for heat dissipation and weight reduction.In addition, the rotary triprade coupling is not decomposed.
