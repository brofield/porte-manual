14. Brush replacement (Starter 0.8kw)



  1. Cut the braSyled wire on the terminal side.




  2. Shave the bra -seiled wire with a file.

    ■ Attention ■
    Since the part to be shaved is narrow, it does not damage the field coil.




  3. Press the plate portion of the replenishment brush to the lead welding side of the terminal.




  4. Solder on the crimp.

    ■ Attention ■
    1. The solder is fully heated and the solder is sufficiently poured into the plate so that it does not flow into the lead wire.
    2. Do not pour the solder into the field coil.



15. Brush replacement (starter 1.0 kW)

□ Participation □
Only two brushes on the plus side (York side) can be replaced, and the negative side (brush side) is replaced with brush holder.

  1. Cut the braSyled wire on the terminal side.




  2. Shave the welding marks of the bra -seiled wire to correct the terminal to the standard dimensions.

    standard:
    1.6mm (thickness)
    5mm (width)

    ■ Attention ■
    Since the part to be shaved is narrow, it does not damage the field coil.




  3. Press the plate portion of the replenishment brush to the lead welding side of the terminal.

19. Starter clutch installation (Starter 0.8kw)

  1. Apply Denso 50 grease to the clutch bush, the spline and the pinoon -top nut.




  2. Attach a clutch and a pinion top nut to the armacha.




  3. Apply Denso 50 grease to the new snap ring and attach it to the groove of Armacha.

  4. Compress the snap ring with the vise.




  5. Hold the outer periphery of the clutch by hand, use a plastic hammer, hit the armacha lightly, and move the pinoN -top nuts on the snapping.

  6. Attach a pinion stop nut (outa).



20. Starter clutch installation (starter 1.0 kW)

  1. Apply Denso 50 grease to the clutch bush, the spline and the pinoon -top nut.




  2. Attach a clutch and a pinion top nut to the planet carrier shaft.

  3. Apply Denso 50 grease to the snap ring and attach it to the planet carrier shaft groove.




  4. Compress the snap ring with the vise.




  5. Hold the outer periphery of the clutch with your hand, use a plastic hammer to lightly hit the planet carrier shaft, and move the pinion top nut on the snapping.




  6. Apply Denso 50 grease to each hit surface of the pinnion driver's housing, clutch, and moving studs.

  7. Attach a pinoon drama to the clutch.




  8. Attach a shock absorber bearing by matching the notches of the drive housing and the convex part of the shock absorber bearing.
