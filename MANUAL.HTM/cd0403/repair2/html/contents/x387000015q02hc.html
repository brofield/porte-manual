Circuit description


A triple catalyst is used to efficiently purify CO, HC, and NOX in exhaust gas.The three -primary catalyst works most efficiently near theoretical air -fuel ratio.Therefore, if the engine is not accurately controlled near the theory air -fuel ratio, CO, HC, and NOX cannot be efficiently purified.Oxygen sensor is installed for this control.The engine control so that the output voltage changes suddenly when the oxygen concentration in the exhaust gas is discharged when a normal combustion is performed near the theoretical air -fuel ratio, and the air -fuel ratio control can be performed accurately.Sending a signal to the competition.When the air -fuel ratio becomes thinner, the oxygen concentration in the exhaust gas increases, and the oxygen sensor sends information that the air -fuel ratio is thin in the engine control compilation.(Small power activation: <0.45V) The information that the air -fuel ratio becomes thicker, the oxygen concentration in the exhaust gas is reduced, and the oxygen sensor sends information that the air -fuel ratio is intense in the engine control composer.(Large power activation:> 0.45V) The engine control compilation determines whether the air -fuel ratio is strong or thin due to the oxygen sensor of the oxygen sensor, and to control the injection.The sensor element uses a zirconium element.The zirconium element is made of platinum plating zirconium and contains integrated overheating components.The inside of the dirkonium element is in the outside air, and the outside is touched by exhaust gas, which generates a faint voltage when there is a significant difference between the exhaust and the oxygen concentration of the outside air.When heated, the sensor becomes very efficient.If the temperature of the exhaust is low, the sensor does not generate a useful voltage signal without heating.Therefore, the oxygen sensor has a heater that warms the zirconium element, and the engine control computer uses a duty cycle approach to regulate supplementary heating to regulate the average current of the zirconium element.
