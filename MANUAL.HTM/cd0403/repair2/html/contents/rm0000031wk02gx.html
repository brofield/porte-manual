Diag detection conditions
  1. Kunen Hena outputs a voltage almost proportional to the air -fuel ratio.Based on this output voltage, the engine control computers judge the air -fuel ratio of the air -fuel ratio, and controls feedback.
    When the engine control computer determines that the response of Kuunenhusensa to the change in the air -fuel ratio is slower than the judgment value, the diag code is set and the check engine warnin gland lamp is turned on.

DTC detection conditions
Diagnostic condition
When all the following conditions are available
  1. Under control of air -fuel feedback
  2. Kunen Hesensa Activate
  3. The engine speed is less than 4000 rpm
  4. Engine load is 10 to 70%
  5. Inhaled air amount is 6 to 20 g/sec
  6. Engine cooled water temperature 80 ° C or more
  7. Not fuel cut
  8. The slot valve is not fully closed
Abnormal state
The response of Kunen Hena Sensa is slower than the judgment value
Abnormal period
10 seconds or more

2 trips

After the engine warm -up, the engine control computer controls air -fuel feedback to maintain the air -fuel ratio at the theoretical level.In addition, an active air -fuel ratio control is performed for about 30 seconds or more after the preliminary conditions are aligned to measure the Kunen Hen Hentasasa reaction rate.During the active air -fuel ratio control, the engine control computer forcibly increases or decreases the amount of injection computers, and measures the reaction rate of Kunenshen Henta.The engine control computer receives a signal from the Kunen Hena Sensa while controlling the active air -fuel ratio, and calculates the level of Kunenshena Sensa by that signal.If the Kunen Hen Hen Sensa Response rate is less than the threshold, the engine control computer determines it as a defect, sets a diag code, and lights the check warorne lamp.

Inspection procedure


□ Participation □
By implementing [fuel injection] in the active test, the problem can be identified.This active test can increase the amount of fuel injection of -12.5%or+24.8%.


ST
09991-70201

  1. Connect SST (Tascan) to DLC3.
  2. Start the engine, turn off all accessories switches, and warm up until the engine cooled water temperature is stabilized.(Engine cooling water temperature is 75 ° C or more)
  3. Hold the engine speed at 2500 RPM for about 3 minutes to warm each sensor.
  4. According to the SST (Tascan) screen display, select [Active Test] - [fuel injection amount], and select [ECU Data Monitor] - [A/F sensor voltage B1S1] and [O2 sensor voltage B1S2].
  5. Idling and read the output voltage of Kunenshena and oxygen sensor when the fuel injection amount is increased or decreased by the active test.

□ Participation □
  1. If the output voltage of the Kunenhe -sensa remains about 3.3 V in any state, including the above state, there is a possibility that the Kuunen Hena has a disconnected circuit.(This occurs even when Kuunen Hena Harter is the opening route.)
  2. If the output voltage of Kunen Hena is about 3.8 V or 2.8 V or less in any state (see the defective state of the illustration), there may be a short -circuit circuit in the Kunen Hena.
  3. The engine control computer stops fuel injection during the deceleration (fuel cut).This causes a lean state, resulting in a temporary increase in the Kunen Hena output voltage.
  4. When driving a vehicle:
    When concentrating the fuel, the output voltage of Kunenhe Sensa may be less than 2.8 V.The vehicle is transmitted to the sudden acceleration of the accelerator pedal when the accelerator pedal is completely stepped.Kunen Hena is working normally.
  5. Kunen Hena is a current output component, so the current is converted to a voltage inside the engine control computer.Certain voltage results are displayed when the voltage is measured by the Kunen Henkona or the engine control computer connector.
