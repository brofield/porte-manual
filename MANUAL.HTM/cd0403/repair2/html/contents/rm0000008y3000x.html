Circuit explanation


The inlet air tempara chasensa is provided in an intake air floometer and senses the inhaled air temperature.The resistance value of the thermista incorporated into the inlet air tempalacha sensor changes due to changes in inhaled air temperature.The lower the inhalation air temperature, the greater the resistance of the thermista, and the higher the higher the resistance, the lower the resistance value.The inlet air tempara chasensa is connected to the engine controlled compilation, and a 5V power supply voltage is supplied to the inlet air tempara chenza via the resistor from the THA terminal of the engine control compilation.In other words, since the resistor R and the inlet air tempara chena are connected in series, the resistance value changes due to changes in inhaled air temperature, and the potential of the THA terminal also changes.Based on this signal, the engine control compiler increases the amount of fuel injection during cold and improves driverability.
