    3. Precautions during work


      1. Attach a wristband to prevent static electricity on your wrist.

      2. Fix the ground clip of the conductive mat to the ground point of the building or workbench.

      3. Fix the earth clip of a wristband for static electricity to prevent static electricity to the predetermined part of the conductive mat.

      4. To prevent damage to the electronic device, when attaching and detaching the main unit or peripheral device, work is performed after removing the battery power.

      5. Never touch the electronic components on the board or the IC pin.

      6. At the time of work, the clothing does not approach or contact the product.



      2. Notes on refrigerant HFC-134A (R134A)



      1. About compatibility


        1. Parts and compressor oils that make up the refrigerant cycle are not compatible between the HFC-134A (R134A) system and the conventional R12 system.




      2. About refrigerant HFC-134A (R134A)


        1. The refrigerant always uses HFC-134A (R134A).

          ■ Attention ■
          1. In the normal handling method, it is non -toxic and harmless, but can be changed to harmful gas under naked fire or high -temperature conditions (tobacco, stoves, etc.).
          2. Pay attention to ventilation and do not work in a closed place.
          3. Gas lying out with a harder torch leak tester will never be performed.

          □ Participation □
          The manifold gauge is dedicated to HFC-134A (R134A), and it is prevented from misleading.




      3. About compressor oil


        1. The compressor oil always uses ND-Oil8.

          □ Participation □
          Using a compressor oil for R12 for the HFC-134A (R134A) system, there is a risk of locking a compressor due to poor lubrication, and a refrigerant gas leaning due to swelling of rubber parts.Conversely, using a compressor oil for HFC-134A (R134A) in the R12 system reduces the durability of the A/C system.

        2. Compressor oil for HFC-134A (R134A) has high moisture absorption, so if you remove A/C parts, it is immediately cut off with plugs, vinyl tape, etc.

        3. Compressor oil for HFC-134A (R134A) does not scatter oil because it has a negative effect on acrylic resin.




      4. About O -ring


        1. The O-ring always uses an O-ring for HFC-134A (R134A).

          □ Participation □
          O-rings for HFC-134A (R134A) and O-rings for R12 have different materials, wire diameter, etc., so if used incorrectly, gas may cause leaning.

      5. Precautions during vacuum drawing work


        1. To do a vacuum drawing, attach the electromagnetic valve adapter to a vacuum pump for R12 and work.

          □ Participation □
          If you leave it after vacuuming without using an electromagnetic valve adapter, the oil (for R12) in the vacuum pump may flow backward and may flow into the HFC-134A (R134A) system.




      6. Fireworks greasy lying out inspection


        1. Inspection of the refrigerant gas and checking using the halogenery questtector (ND part number 95146-00060).

          □ Participation □
          1. The conventional harder torch leak-tester cannot be used because HFC-134A (R134A) does not contain chlorine.
          2. The conventional electric gas leak tester is also smaller than the molecule of the R12 because the molecule of HFC-134A (R134A) may not be detected.

        2. Inspection of the refrigerant gas lying out on the IG switch off.

          □ Participation □
          1. If you do it during the engine rotation, the gasses are diluted and difficult to detect due to the wind of a fan and blower.
          2. The refrigerant pressure in the cooler unit decreases, making it harder for gas to lose.
          3. The electric gas leak tester reacts to the sudden change of humidity due to the wet air from the drain hose (especially the highly sensitive HFC-134A (R134A) compatible type is easy to respond).



        3 .. Precautions for detachment work

        1. Processing when refrigerant gas comes into your eyes


          1. Do not rub your eyes with your hands or handkerchiefs.

          2. Receive a professional ophthalmologist.

        2. Care for handling refrigerant gas (service can)


          1. Do not heat the service can.If the service can is heated directly or in the boiling water, the pressure in the can will be abnormally high, and there is a risk that the can will burst.If you have to warm up a service can, use hot water or less of 40 ° C or less.

          2. Do not drop or hit the service can.

          3. During the engine, do not open the high -pressure valve of the manifold gauge.When the engine rotation medium and high -pressure valve is opened, the high -pressure gas may flow back into the service can and the cans may burst.

          4. Do not handle service cans near the face.Also, be sure to wear protective glasses.




        3. Attachment of piping


          1. The O -ring always uses a new one.

            □ Participation □
            1. Be sure to use an O-ring for HFC-134A (R134A).
            2. When removing the O -ring, use a soft one such as a yakko so that the piping is not damaged.

          2. Apply the compressor oil (ND-Oil8) to the O-ring.

          3. The hose piping is naturally indeed, twisted, or forcibly bent.

          4. If a foreign substance such as lint is attached to the O -ring, it will cause a refrigerant gas leakage, so work without using gloves.
