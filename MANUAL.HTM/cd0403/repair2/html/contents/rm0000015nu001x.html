Function explanation


  1. Output these codes when the ferrous sensor tip, foreign substance attached to the sensor rotor and the rotation of the rotor.
  2. It may be detected by the connector terminal of the speed sensor circuit or a defect of the wire harness.

At the speed of 20 km/h or more, the pulse waveform abnormal state of the speed sensor signal continues for 5 seconds or more
  1. Sensor rotor abnormality (foreign substance attached, tooth loss)
  2. Foreign substance attached to speed sensor
  3. Speed ​​sensor disconnection or short
  4. Wire harness disconnection or short
  5. Failure of connectors, deformation of terminals, corrosion
  6. Speed ​​sensor circuit wiring resistance
  7. Brake actuator ASSY

Inspection procedure

Step 1Speed ​​sensor output waveform inspection
SST
09991-70200



  1. Using the oscilloscope function of SST (TASCAN), the wire harness side connector of the actuator Asset Assembly 31 (FR+) ← → 30 (FR-) 9 (FL+) ← → 8 (FL-) or 33(RR+) ← → 34 (RR-) Check the waveform between the terminals and 11 (RL+) ← → 12 (RL-).




    1. Osciroscope waveform

      standard
      The same voltage waveform is output to the left and right wheels, and no noise is mixed in the waveform.

      ■ Attention ■
      1. The higher the vehicle speed (wheel rotation), the shorter the cycle, and the output voltage amplitude increases.
      2. If the gap between the speed sensor and the rotor is large, the output voltage is lower.
      3. If a foreign substance adheres to the speed sensor, the output voltage of the corresponding wheel increases.

      □ Participation □
      1. Instrument set 1V/Div, 2ms/Div
      2. Measurement condition car speed is about 30km/h

  1. Make a light vibration by sprinkling connectors and harnesses to check that there is no change in the waveform.

    standard
    There is no change in the waveform

    □ Participation □
    If the waveform changes due to vibration, it is considered that there is an abnormality in the connector or wire harness at the relevant part.
