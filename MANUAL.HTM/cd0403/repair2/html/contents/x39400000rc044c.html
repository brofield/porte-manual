Circuit explanation



  1. A direct ignition system (S-TDI) is adopted.S-TDI enhances ignition accuracy, suppresses the loss of high voltage, and enhances the overall reliability of the ignition system.S-TDI ignites one cylinder with one ignition coil.The engine control computer determines the ignition time and outputs a ignition signal (IGT) to each cylinder.Using the ignition signal (IGT), the engine control computer turns on and off the power transistor in the igniter, which turns the current to the primary coil on and off.When the current to the primary coil is cut off, the secondary coil generates a reverse power, and the voltage is applied to the spark plug and sparks in the cylinder.At the same time, the igniter sends a ignition confirmation signal (IGF) to the engine control computer.

Inspection procedure


■ Attention ■
Check the fuse of this circuit before performing trouble shooting.


□ Participation □
  1. When DTC P0351/14 is displayed, check the ignition coil No.1 (No. 1 cylinder) circuit.
  2. When DTC P0352/15 is displayed, check the ignition coil No.1 (No. 2 cylinder) circuit.
  3. When DTC P0353/14 is displayed, check the ignition coil No.1 (No. 3 cylinder) circuit.
  4. When DTC P0354/15 is displayed, check the ignition coil No.1 (No. 4 cylinder) circuit.
  5. Read freeze frame data using SST (Tascan).The freeze -frame data records a part of the engine operation state in the event of a problem, which helps you take a trouble shot.
  6. The following shows the No. 1 cylinder inspection method.If a problem other than the No.1 cylinder has occurred, check the corresponding cylinder circuit with reference to the following procedure.
