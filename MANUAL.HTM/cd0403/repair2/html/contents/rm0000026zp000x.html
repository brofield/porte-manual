    1. The mounting direction is not wrong.
    2. Do not damage the oil seal.

4. Power steering rack installation

  1. Cut the rack cover tube so that it is about 30mm longer than the tip of the rack while covering the tooth surface and port.

    ■ Attention ■
    Since the rack cover tube is used to protect an oil seal, the surface damage on the tube may cause oil leakage.For this reason, it is confirmed that there is no damage such as burrs and cracks on the tube surface before shrinking.




  2. Cut the paper with a thickness of a newspaper to the same length as the rack cover tube with a width that covers the tooth surface of the steering rack.

    □ Participation □
    Use paper to prevent rack teeth from remaining in the tube during contracts.




  3. Fill the chassis grease special on the valley of the teeth of the steering rack.

  4. Set the paper so that the steering rack is covered.

  5. The rack cover tube is inserted to the position where the port is covered in the folded direction as shown in the figure.




  6. Using the back side of the teeth, the steering rack is applied diagonally on the outlet of the dryer as shown in the figure, and the dryer in the rack cover tube is pushed out to the B side, and the dryer is moved to section B.Controls the rack cover tube.

    ■ Attention ■
    When the tooth surface is used, the dental shape remains on the tube and the tube is less likely to come off, so the back side is used.

    □ Participation □
    The dryer is easy to work on the outlet squeezed because the tube folds are contracted on one side.




  7. Only the rack cover tube rotates 180 °.

  8. Use the back of the teeth to contract the rack cover tube.

  9. Draw the paper.

    □ Participation □
    If the paper is torn, remove the rack cover tube and remove the paper.When the tube is installed, it is securely inserted to the position to cover the port.




  10. While turning the steering rack, only warm the tip C, and quickly twist the rack cover tube into the screw hole of the rack end.

    ■ Attention ■
    When the tooth surface is heated, only the tube C is heated, so only the tip part C is heated.

  11. Confirm that there is no protrusion at the tip and there is no edge.

    □ Participation □
    If there is a protrusion on the tip and an edge, warm the tip again and use a ballpoint pen to push it into the screw hole.

  12. Confirm that there is no scratch on the rack cover tube surface.

  13. Apply power steering fluid to the steering rack rack cover tube and piston ring.

  14. Attach the steering rack to the rack housing and remove the rack cover tube.

    ■ Attention ■
    1. Does not hurt the piston ring.
    2. The rack cover tube cannot be reused.
    3. After removing the rack cover tube, the rack overstroke may damage the oil seal on the tooth surface and port, so the steering rack is not moved more than necessary until the control valve is attached.

13. Power steering rack guide installation



  1. Apply a chassis grease special to the surface of the rack guide.

  2. Attach the rack guide to the rack housing.

  3. Clean the spring cap and rack housing screw.

  4. Attach the rack guide spacer and rack guide spring to the rack housing.

  5. Apply Adherive 1344 to the screw of the spring cap and temporarily tighten it.



14. Comprehensive plost adjustment

  1. Tighten the spring cap using a straight hexagon wrench 19.

    Reference value
    T = 25 n { 250 kgf / cm }




  2. Use a straight hexagon wrench 19 to loosen the spring cap about 12 °.

  3. Put the left and right racks to the steering rack.

    □ Participation □
    Full stroke of the steering rack without a rack end may damage the oil seal, so the rack end is attached.




  4. Use SST to make the steering rack a full stroke once-2 times and blend in the steering rack.

    ST
    09616 -0011

  5. Use a straight hexagon wrench 19 to loosen the spring cap until the rack guide spring does not work.




  6. When the control valve is rotated using the SST, the spring cap is gradually tightened so that the rotating pre -road becomes a reference value.

    ST
    09616 -0011

    Reference value
    T = 1-2 · M {11-17kgf · cm}
