Circuit explanation



  1. A triple catalyst is used to efficiently purify CO, HC, and NOX in exhaust gas.The three -primary catalyst works most efficiently near theoretical air -fuel ratio.For this reason, if the engine is not accurately controlled near the theory air -fuel ratio, CO, HC, and Nox cannot be efficiently purified.Kunen Hena is attached for this control.Kunen Hena has the property of output voltage in proportion to the air -fuel ratio, and based on its voltage, the engine control computer controls the air -fuel ratio.A zirconium element is used for the sensor element.The zirconium element is made of platinum plating zirconia and contains integrated overheating components.The inside of the dirkonium element is in the outside air, and the outside is touched by exhaust gas, which generates a faint voltage when there is a significant difference between the exhaust and the oxygen concentration of the outside air.When heated, the sensor becomes very efficient.If the temperature of the exhaust is low, the sensor does not generate a useful voltage signal without heating.Therefore, Kunen Hena has a heater that warms the zirconium element, and the engine control computer uses a duty cycle approach to regulate supplementary heating and adjusts the average current of the zirconium element.
